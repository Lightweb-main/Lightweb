Imaginary Numbers

Introduction

Imaginary numbers are unreal numbers which cannot be accurately shown with positive and negative values. An example of an imaginary number is the square root of negative 1…

-1 = ???

There is no root to a negative number because no number multiplied by itself can equal a negative number. Meaning that the answer to this equation isn’t real. This is why the letter, “i” or “iota” (or the imaginary unit) and its value is in fact the square root of negative 1…

-1 = i

Imaginary numbers are usually numbers as coefficients…

4 i = 4 -1 -3 i = -3 -1 9 i = 9 -1

Another way to interpret imaginary numbers is that i squared equals a negative number…

i 2 = negative number
