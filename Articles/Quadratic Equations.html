Quadratic Equations

Introduction

Quadratic Equations are equations with letters or known as unknown letters or variables. Quadratic Equations are actually very similar to Linear Equations with the involvement of variables within them but unlike Linear Equations Quadratic equations have exponents in them like this one…

5x2 + 2x + 3 = 0

A couple of notes here would be the equation equals 0 which is part of Quadratic Equations. You may also notice that there are instead three numbers all added up to equal 0. In standard form, Quadratic Equations are always…

ax2 + bx + c = 0

A, B, and C are all known values since coefficients are next to them. If you didn’t know, x is the variable in this problem. Variables are unknown numbers (usually appearing as letters) and or the value that is usually figured out. Coefficients are numerals that are on the left side of variables, they usually appear as a number greater than one, this means that there are more than one x. In a quadratic equation, A cannot be zero, otherwise the equation would not be quadratic....

2x = x * 2

When quadratic equations are graphed. The line would appear curved upside down or straight up. This is because of the exponents since they can affect a line much more than just a simple number... The Quadratic Formula is the formula used to figure out the two answers in a Quadratic Equation… x = - b ± b2 - 4 a c 2 a Let's Break down this formula. We can see that A, B, and C are all a part of the formula. A is always the first coefficient, You might have noticed the weird plus and minus sign right next to the square root and the -b. This is the plus-minus sign that is used in Quadratic Equations to act as + and -. This creates two different solutions for quadratic problems. Both of these solutions are also called roots. So let's say we had a quadratic problem like this…

6x2 - 5x + 1 = 0

Negative 5b squared is a, 2b is b, and 1 is c. So to find the variable in this equation being b, we would have to apply the quadratic equation… In this equation, 6 is a, -5 is b, and since 1 is on its own, 1 is c. Remember this, ±, this is a plus-minus sign which can act as a plus or minus sign. But first let's deal with the square root of 1. Since no number multiplied by itself can equal 1, the square root of 1 is 1… x = 5 ± 1 12 First we will do 5 plus 1… x = 5 + 1 12   =  6 12 And now that we have our final fraction, 6 over 12 is a half which is 0.5. x = 6 12 = 0.5 Now we will find another solution for this problem.
