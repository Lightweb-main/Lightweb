Population and Sample Standard Deviation

Introduction

In statistics, standard deviation has two formulas, both for population and sample, both have different formulas.

Population ≠ Sample

In statistics, population and sample are not the same thing and telling them apart is crucial in statistics.

Population

A population includes every number in data. An example would be…

1, 2, 3, 4, 5

This is the population of the data because it includes every value in the data.

Sample

A sample is a subset of the population, in other words, it contains some of the values but not all of it…

1, 3, 4

In this sample, the numbers 1, 3 and 4 are present, but 2 and 5 are not here. Samples are often solved when the population is too large, complicated or difficult. Samples are used to break down the population and make solving easier.

Standard Deviation with Populations

The formula for standard deviation with populations is… σ 2 = 1 N ∑ ( xi - μ )2

σ = ∑ ( xi - μ )2 N In this formula, is the standard deviation of the data, N is the amount of numbers. is the summation of all the values… An example of standard deviation would be…

Population:

16, 20, 56, 72, 103

In the population, five values are present… σ 2 = 1 5 ∑ ( xi - μ )2

And the sum between all the numbers is…

16 + 20 = 36

36 + 56 = 92

92 + 72 = 164

164 + 103 = 267

267 and divided by 5 is…

267/5 = 53.4

The mean of the population is 53.4…

μ = 53.4

σ 2 = 1 5 ∑ ( xi - 53.4 )2

Now we must take every value from the population and subtract all of them by 53.4…

16 - 53.4 = -37.4

20 - 53.4 = -33.4

56 - 53.4 = 2.6

72 - 53.4 = 18.6

103 - 53.4 = 49.6

Now we must square each sum…

(-37.4)2 = 1398.76

(-33.4)2 = 1115.56

(2.6)2 = 6.76

(18.6)2 = 345.96

(49.6)2 = 2460.16

Now we must add them together…

1398.76 + 1115.56 = 2514.32

2514.32 + 6.76 = 2521.08

2521.08 + 345.96 = 2867.04

2867.04 + 2460.16 = 5327.2

And 5229 divided by 5…

5327.2/5 = 1065.44

σ = 1065.44

The standard deviation of the population is the square root of 1065.44 or it squared equals 1065.44… σ = 1065.44

σ 2 = 1065.44

Sample Standard Deviation

In standard deviation, samples have a different formula for it… s = ∑ ( xi - x ¯ )2 n - 1 Not only the values are shown differently, the sum of the squares are divided by the numbers divided by the number of observations minus 1. An example of a sample standard deviation problem would be…

Sample #1

20, 56

Sample #2

16, 72

We can find both of the sample standard deviations using the formula… s1 = ∑ ( xi - x ¯ )2 n - 1 s2 = ∑ ( xi - x ¯ )2 n - 1

76/2 = 38

88/2 = 44

Subtracting all the values…

20 - 38 = -18    16 - 44 = -28

56 - 38 = 18   72 - 44 = 28

Squaring all the values…

(-18)2 = 324

(18)2 = 324


(-28)2 784

(28)2 = 784

Adding up the values…

324 + 324 = 648

784 + 784 = 1568

s1 = 648 2 - 1 s2 = 1568 2 - 1

s1 = 648 s2 = 1568 The first sample’s standard deviation is the square root of 648, the second sample’s standard deviation is the square root of 1568.

Here are both of the formulas…

Population Standard Deviation

σ = ∑ ( xi - μ )2 N

Sample Standard Deviation

s = ∑ ( xi - x ¯ )2 n - 1
