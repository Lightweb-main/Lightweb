Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion

Introduction

Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion are both measures used to describe and summerize data. Each measure shows a different value in data.

Measures of Central Tendency

Measures of Central Tendency are measures that represent a central or typical value in a dataset. Measures of Central Tendency include Mean, Median, and Mode since they all either measure the central (median and mean) or the typical value (mode).

Measures of Dispersion

Measures of Dispersion are measures that represent the spread, scatter, or varibility of a datset. These include, Range, Variance, Standard Deviation (SD), Mean Deviation/Mean Absolute Deviation, and Quartile Deviation. All of these calculate the distance between two values unlike measures of central tendency focus on one central/typical value.
