Antiderivatives

Introduction

Antiderivatives (also called, reverse derivatives, primitive integrals, and primitive functions) are calculus equations that are the complete opposite of derivatives hence the name.

There are differences and similarities between antiderivatives and derivatives. First antiderivatives come from integrals which have integral signs whereas derivatives come from ddx notation…



Antiderivatives: ∫3x dx Derivatives: ddx3x

Solving Antiderivatives

To find antiderivatives, you simply do the opposite for derivatives. For example the power rule turns from this…

ddx = nxn - 1

To this…

∫x dx = xn + 1n + 1

For example…

∫3x4 dx

First we add one to the exponent…

3x4 + 1 3x5

Then we divide by the new exponent…

3x55

The antiderivative of 3x4 is 3x55.

Antiderivatives vs Integrals

Antiderivatives are also associated with indefinite integrals as they are pretty much the same except antiderivatives include +C because C represents the constant of integration...
