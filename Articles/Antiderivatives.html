Antiderivatives

Introduction

Antiderivatives (also called, reverse derivatives, primitive integrals, and primitive functions) are calculus equations that are the complete opposite of derivatives hence the name.

There are differences and similarities between antiderivatives and derivatives. First antiderivatives come from integrals which have integral signs whereas derivatives come from d/dx notation…

Solving Antiderivatives

To find antiderivatives, you simply do the opposite for derivatives. For example the power rule turns from this…

d/dx = nxn - 1


To this…

∫x dx = xn + 1 / n + 1


For example…

∫3x4 dx


First we add one to the exponent…

3x4 + 1

3x5

Then we divide by the new exponent…

3x5/ 5

The antiderivative of 3x4 is 3x5 / 5.

Antiderivatives vs Integrals

Antiderivatives are also associated with indefinite integrals as they are pretty much the same except antiderivatives include +C because C represents the constant of integration...

Indefinite Integral:
∫3x4 dx

Antiderivatives:
3x5 / 5 + C

Antiderivative Notation

Antiderivatives are shown in numerous ways. Antiderivatives are shown as a capital F…

Summary

Glossary:

Antiderivative: A function when differentiated returns the original function. Also part of a set of itself called an indefinite integral.

Derivatives: The slope of a tangent line of a point; an instantaneous rate of change.

Integral: The area under a curve.

+ C (Constant of Integration): Only appearing in indefinite integrals, an arbitrary value the set of all the antiderivatives of a function.
