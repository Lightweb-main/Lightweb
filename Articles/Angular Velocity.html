Angular Velocity

Introduction

Angular Velocity quantifies how fast an object is moving around an axis, or a center point and is expressed or shown in rad/s or radians per second. Similar to the formula for regular velocity, angular velocity is angular displacement over time...

Angular Velocity  =  Angular Displacement Time Displacement

But in physics, the formula for angular velocity looks different...



ω  =  Δθ Δt

So the weird w at the start of the equation is the lowercase version of the greek letter, omega which represents angular velocity. Also the triangle on the top and bottom of the fraction is an uppercase delta which represents the difference between two values, the weird oval is an lowercase theta which represents an angle. Lastly the t at the bottom represents time.

Finding Angular Velocity Using Angle Displacement

In order to find angular velocity, you need to know the difference in time and the angle displacement or the difference between the final angle and the intial angle.

Example #1

A fan is spinning at 60 RPM, find it's angular velocity in rad/s.

So the angle displacement is 60 revolutions and it's rotating that fast in 1 minute, or 60 seconds. So by dividing both sides by 60, we can say that the fan was spinning 1 revolution every second...



60 rev 60 seconds =  1 rev per second

So the fan can be simplified to spin 1 revolution per second, the last step is to convert revolutions into radians...

Since 1 revolution = 6.283 rad

So from the formula above, the fan is spinning 6.283 rad/s or radians per second.

Example #2

A wheel spins at 450 Revolutions in 15 seconds. Calculate it's angular velocity and express it in rad/s.

First divide the angle displacement (450 revolutions) with the time displacement (15 seconds)...



450 rev 15 seconds =  30 rev per second

So the angular velocity is 30 revolutions, now we must convert it into radians...

30 revolutions --> 188.495 rads

So the wheel is spinning at 188.495 radians per second or 30 revolutions per second.

Summary

Glossary: Omega (ω): In physics, represents angular velocity. Delta (Δ): In math, represents the change of a variable. Theta (θ): In math, represents an angle.
Formulas: Angular Velocity: ω  =  Δθ Δt Revolutions (full turns) to Radians:
1 rev = 2π rad ≈ 6.283 rad
