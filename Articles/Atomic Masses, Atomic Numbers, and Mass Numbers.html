Atomic Masses, Atomic
Numbers, and Mass Numbers

Atomic Numbers and Mass Numbers are both identities of certain elements with Atomic Mass being the mass of a single atom (with isotopes included). With every element, there is an atomic number, atomic mass and a mass number.

Atomic Numbers

The atomic number of an element is the amount of protons that are present within one atom of an element. In the periodic table, atomic numbers are shown at the top left corner of an element...

So for example Oxygen (O) has 8 protons.

Atomic Masses

The atomic masses of an element is the weighted average mass of an atom in an element. In the periodic table, the atomic mass is the number at the bottom of the element either measured in amu (atomic mass unit) or in daltons (daltons)...

So in this example, the element, "iron" or shown as Fe has an atomic mass of 54.845, meaning that one atom has a mass of 54.845 amu (atomic mass unit) or 54.845 Da (daltons)...

1 amu = 1 dalton

Mass Numbers

The mass number of an element is the combined amount of protons and neutrons in a single atom of an element. Mass numbers are often shown with the notation...

X Z A

So this is represented in nuclide notation. So for example...

O 8 16

In this example, the letter to the right of the two smaller numbers is called the element symbol (X), it basically tells us the chemical, so for this, "O" stands for Oxygen...



O 8 16

The upper number is called the Mass Number (A). The mass number is the sum of the neutrons and protons in an atom. In an atom, the mass number cannot be smaller than the amount of protons or neutrons...



O 8 16

The bottom number is the atomic number (Z) or the amount of protons there are in a single atom...



O 8 16

With the atomic number and mass number known, you can find the amount of neutrons in an atom of an element...

A = 16, Z = 8 Amount of Neutrons = 16 - 8 Amount of Neutrons = 8

Quick Nuclide Notation Explanation

Mass Number = Z Atomic Nunber = A Element Symbol = X X Z A

Summary

Glossary: Atomic Number: Amount of Protons in an atom of an element. Atomic Masses: The weighted mass of an atom, inlcuding isotopes. Mass Number: The sum of protons and neutrons, called nucleons.
Formulas: None
