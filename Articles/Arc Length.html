Arc Length

Introduction

An Arc in mathematics is a portion of a complete circle, not a circle, but a part of it. All arcs have length, known as Arc Length and more arc length means that the arc covers more of the circumference of the circle.

Calculating Arc Length

To calculate an arc's length, you want to know the central angle which is the angle at the center of the circle that the arc encompasses. You may also know the radius of the circle which is the length of the line that stretches to the halfway point of the circle. The formula to find arc length is...

While the angle is expressed in Radians

Arc Length = r * θ

While the angle is expressed in degrees



θ 360  * 2πr

Or



θ * π 180  * r

Finding Arc Length With Central Angle And With Radius

Examples

Example #1

An arc encompasses 1/8th of a full circle's circumference while the circle itself has a radius of 25m. Calculate the length of the arc.

What we know from the problem is the central angle which is 1/8th of the circumference of a full circle, which is 45 degrees. And we can also see that the radius of the circle is 25m or 25 meters. So by plugging in the values...

45° * π 180  * 25m



45° * π 180 ≈ 0.785



45° * π 180 * 25 = 0.78539 * 25 = 19.635m

So the arc's length that covers 45 degrees of a circle that has a radius of 25m is about 19.635m.

Example #2

An arc covers about 1.5π radians of a circle, which has a radius of 45m. Calculate the length of the arc.

What we know from the problem is the central angle is about 1.5%pi; radians, or about 270 degrees. This is true because 1 rad * π equals 180, so 1 rad * pi plus 0.5 rad * pi would equal 270 degrees. What we also know is that the radius of the circle is 45m. So by plugging in the values, the length of the arc is...

1.5 rad * r



1.5 * 45m



Arc Length = 67.5m

From the formula, Arc Length = θ * r, the length of the arc is 67.5 meters.

Finding Arc Length Using A Central Angle Without Radius

To find the length of an arc without the radius, instead calculate the sector area which can be using the formula...

In degrees...



Sector Area = (θ / 360°) * πr²

In radians



Sector Area = (θ / 360°) * πr²

Examples

Example #1

A rainbow in a storm has been spotted, it's shape is that of an arc that is 1/4th of a circumference of a complete circle, the area of it's sector is about 120m2. Calculate it's length.

What we know from this problem is that the central angle is about 1/4th of 360 degrees, or 360 / 4 degrees, or 90 degrees. The only value unknown is the area of the sector...

Sector Area = (90° / 360°) * πr²



120 = (1/4) * πr²



120 = (1/4) * πr²



480 = πr²



152.788 = r²



r = 12.36m;

So by isolating r on the right and the sector area on the left, we can see that the radius is 12.36m2. Now we can solve it normally...

90° * π 180  * 12.36m



90° * π 180  * 3.09m² = 1.5707 * 12.36m



90° * π 180  * 3.09m² = 1.5707 * 12.36m



90° * π 180  * 3.09m² = 19.413m

The final length of the arc is 19.413 meters.
