Radius, Diameter & Circumference

Introduction

Before you read the text below you have to remember that, Radius, Diameter and Circumference are NOT the same. Sure they are relative to each other but they are different from each other.

Radius of a Circle

Radius is the halfway point of a circle. It is used to calculate the Diameter, Area, and Volume of a circle. Now to better depict this, first draw a circle... Now imagine a line that just went halfway through the circle like this... That line that just went about halfway through the circle is the radius of the circle. Now there are a few formulas to calculate a circle's radius. One is to divide the diameter by two, by doing this you would get the radius...

Radius of a Semicircle

The radius of a semi circle can be calculated through its base. The part where the two separate semi circles intersect is the base of both of the two semi circles. That line is the diameter and a diameter is twice the radius. So dividing the diameter by two equals the radius...

Area of a Semicircle

The area of a semi circle can be calculated in two ways, you can get the area of the whole circle and then divide it by 2 to get one of the semi circles area. You can do them individually to get their area. The same formula applies to semi circles but instead of keeping the product as is. After you multiply the radius squared times pi, you divide the product by two since a semi circle occupies 50 percent of the whole circle...

Circumference of a semi circle

The circumference of a semicircle is a little different compared to calculating for a whole circle. Instead you would have to find the diameter of the semi circle. The direction of the split does matter. If the circle is looking like this, then find the radius of it then multiply it by 2, then multiply it by pi...

Diameter of a Circle

The diameter of a circle is the distance from one point on the left all the way to the right. Unlike a chord or radius of a circle. The diameter of a circle is very precise, it is twice the distance of the radius of the circle. And it is found to find the circumference of a circle easily...
