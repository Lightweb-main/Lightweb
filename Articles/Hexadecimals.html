Hexadecimals

Introduction

Hexadecimals are numbers that follow the traditional digits from 0 to 9 but has six additional digits added, this makes the base of every hexadecimal 16 since unlike the decimal numerical system, hexadecimals also include, "A" to "F"...

Hexadecimals Pattern

Just like how digits exponentially increase depending on where it is, for example, tens are 10 ones, hundreds are 10 tens, and thousands areare 10 hundreds. But in hexadecimal, this is different as the digits are multiplied by 16 every space to the left, for example, now tens are 16 ones, hundreds are 256 ones, and thousands are 4096 ones when converted to decimal...

Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal

To convert a decimal number to a hexadecimal number is to first divide the original number by 16...

182 to Hexadecimal

182 / 16 = 11 remainder 6

So now 182 / 16 equals 11 with a remainder of 6. Next, we divide 11 by 16...

11 / 16 = 0 remainder 11

11 / 16 equals 0 with a remainder of 11. To turn 182 into a hexadecimal, we must place the values from bottom to top, so 11 turns into B and 6 stays the same...

18210 --> B616

So 182 in decimal form is B6 in hexadecimal form.
