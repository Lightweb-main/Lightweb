Angular Velocity with Linear Velocity

Introduction

Angular Velocity and Linear Velocity are both changes in motion in an object. Linear velocity is the amount of displacement over time, this can be confused with speed which is distance over time however both can be used in the same context. Angular velocity however is angle displacement, the circular motion around a fixed axis, over time.



Linear Velocity

Linear velocity, usually displacement over time is calculated using the formula...



Δs Δt

But when we're talking about a motion around a fixed axis, it's instead...



v = rω

The formula states that the linear velocity from angular velocity is the radius of the cirle times the angular velocity.

Exmaples (finding linear velocity from angular velocity)

Example #1

A frisbee is thrown, it's trajectory is estimated to be traveling at about 0.5 rad/s around a circle that has a radius of 10 meters. Calculate it's linear velocity.

What we know from the problem is...

The Angular Velocity - 0.5 rad/s The radius of the circle - 10 meters

With these, we can plug in the values directly into the formula...

v = 10(0.5) m/s v = 5 m/s

Example #2

A car is driven around a circular track as it travels 30 degrees each second and around a circle that is about 60 meters in diameter. Calculate it's linear velocity.

So what we know from this equation is the angular velocity and the diameter of the circle. We must divide the diameter by 2 to get the radius of the circle...

Angular Velocity - 30 deg/s Diameter - 60 meters Radius - 60 meters / 2 Radius - 30 meters

Now that we know the radius, we can convert 30 degrees to radians...



θ * π 180



94.248 180



94.248 180   = 0.5236 rad

Now that we have the angular velocity in radians and we have the radius of the circle, we can plug the values into the formulas to get the linear velocity of the car...

v = (30)(0.5236) v = 15.708

The linear velocity of the car is 15.708.

Examples (finding angular velocity from linear velocity)

To calculate angular velocity from linear velocity, take the formula that is used to calculate linear velocity from angular velocity and divide both sides by r...

v = rω

v r   = ω



ω =    v r

Example #1

The Earth is about 150 million miles away from the sun as it travels at about 67,000 miles per hour. Find the angular velocity of Earth and express it in rads per day

So what we know from the problem is the radius of the circle which is 150 million miles and the linear velocity of Earth...

Radius of the Circle - 150 million miles Linear Velocity - 67,000 miles per hour

From this data, we can calculate the angular velocity of Earth by using the formula, ω = v / r...



ω =    v r



ω =    67,000 m/h 150,000,000 miles



ω = 0.000447 rad/h

Now the final step is to convert te rad/h to rad/day...



ω = 0.000447(24) rad/day



ω = 0.010728 rad/day

So the amount of radians the Earth travels per day around the sun is 0.010728 radians.

Example #2

A ferris wheel is a common theme park ride that consists of seperate carts attached to a giant circle that keeps rotating all the carts at once. A ferris wheel at a theme park has a radius of 20 meters while every individual cart travels 12 meters per minute. Calculate the angular velocity of one cart.

What we know from the problem is the radius of the ferris wheel which is 20 meters, while it also gives us the linear velocity of every individual cart which is 12 meters per minute...

Radius - 20 meters Linear Velocity - 10 meters per minute

First, lets convert 10 meters per minute to meter per second...

Linear Velocity - 12 meters per minute --> 0.2 meters per second

Now we can find out the angular velocity...



ω =    v r



ω =    0.2 meters per second 20



ω = 0.01 rad/s

The final angular velocity of each indivdual cart on the ferris wheel is 0.01 rad/s.

Derivation of the Linear Velocity Formula (from angular velocity)

1. v =   Δs Δt 2. Replacing Δs with arc length v =   rΔθ Δt 3. v =  r Δθ Δt 4. v =  rω

Summary

Glossary: Angular Velocity: A change in angular displacement over time interval. Linear Velocity: A change in position over time interval.
Formulas: Angular Velocity (radians): ω  =  Δθ Δt Angular Velocity (degrees): ω  =  Δ((θ / 180) * π) Δt Linear Velocity: v  =  Δd Δt  =  rω
