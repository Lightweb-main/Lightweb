Negative Exponents & Numbers to the Power of Zero

Introduction

Powers are numbers that are multiplied by themselves a number of times like this one…

23 = 2 * 2 * 2

But the exponent, which is the small number above and to the right of the base can be negative…

2-4

And a base can have an exponent that is 0…

20 = 2 * 0 ???

Numbers to the Power of Zero

When a number is to the power of zero, it doesn’t equal zero since 0 can’t equal any number except itself. The rule when a number is to the power of zero is that it always equals 1 no matter how big or small it is…

20 = 1

1,000,000,000,0000 = 1

0.00000000010 = 1

Even powers to other powers to the power of zero equals 1…

((x3)3000)0 = 1

In conclusion, every number to the power of zero equals 1…

x0 = 1

Number to the Power of Negative Numbers

When a number is to the power of a negative number. It is the reciprocal of the power that has the opposite exponent…

3-3 = 1/33

x-a = 1/xa

So for example 4 to the power of -2…

4-2

This power is the reciprocal of 4 to the power of 2 which is 16…

4-2 = 1/42

= 1/16

So 4 to the power of -2 is 1/16 since 4 to the power of positive 2 is 16 and a power with a negative exponent is the reciprocal of itself with the opposite exponent. Negative exponents can also be shown as a fraction like 5 to the power of -9…

5-9

We can just say that this is 1 divided by 5 to the power of 9…

5-9 = 1 / 59

It can also be used for variables as well…

x8y-9 = x8 / y9

Even with numbers and with variables…

h12t-23104 = h12104 / t23

= 10000h12 / t23

Powers with positive exponents go on the top while the powers with negative exponents go on the bottom.
