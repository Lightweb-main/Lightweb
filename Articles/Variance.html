Variance

Introduction

Variance is the statistical measure of dispersion that quantifies the amount of spread or variability in a dataset. A high variance indicates that the values are widely spread out while a low variance indicates that the values are closely spread out from each other. Calculating the variance is very similar to finding the standard deviation…

σ 2 = 1 N ∑ ( xi - μ )2

s2 = ∑ i = ( xi - x ¯ )2 n - 1

Variance of a Population

An example of finding the Variance of a population is…

1, 3, 5, 6, 10

First we find the average of the dataset…

1 + 3 + 5 + 6 + 10 = 9 + 6 + 10 = 25 25 / 5 = 5

The mean is 5…

σ 2 = 1 N ∑ ( xi - 5 )2

Now we subtract all the values by 5…

1 - 5 = -4 3 - 5 = -2 5 - 5 = 0 6 - 5 = 1 10 - 5 = 5

Now we square each number…

(-4)2 = 16 (-2)2 = 4 (0)2 = 0 (1)2 = 1 (5)2 = 25

Now we add up all the values…

16 + 4 + 0 + 1 + 25 = 20 + 1 + 25 = 46 σ2 = 46 / 5 σ2 = 9.2

The Variance of the dataset is 9.2.

The variance of a sample is a little different from finding the variance of a population…

Subtract…
