Geometric Mean

Introduction

The geometric mean of a set of numbers is all of the numbers multiplied and rooted by the same amount of numbers…

n√ x1 * x2 * xn

General Formula

The general formula for the geometric mean is... A * B

∏ n

∏ - The product of multiple numbers/terms

Essentially the geometric mean is the product of all the numbers rooted by the amount of numbers. Like arithmetic mean, geometric mean is a measure of central tendency, meaning that the answer is affected by all the values and the amount of numbers. For example, let's say that there is a set of numbers and the question specifically tells you to find the geometric mean…

2, 4, 7, 11, 15

First we would have to multiply all the numbers together…

2 * 4 = 8

8 * 7 = 56

56 * 11 = 616

616 * 15 = 9240

Now that the final product of all the numbers is here, we would have to put it in a root… 9240

Now the index of the root is 5 because in the set of numbers there are five numbers in total… 9240 5

This is when you need your calculator… 9240 5

≈ 6.2

The final geometric mean of the set of numbers is 6.2 (rounded).
