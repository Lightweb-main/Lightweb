Covariance

Introduction

The Covariance between two different variables is the quantification of the linear relationship between them. A positive cova riance indicates that as one variable increases, the other tends to do so as well. A negative covariance indicates that as one v ariable increases, the other tends to decrease. Zero covariance indicates no linear relationship between the two variables.

Formula

The formula for finding the Covariance between two variables is…



Population: cov(x, y) = Σ ( x i - x ) ( y i - x ) N

Sample: cov(x, y) = Σ ( x i - x ) ( y i - x ) N - 1
