Populations

Introduction

Around the Earth, there are many, many species. These species can either be on the brink of extinction or have billions of it around the entire world. A species has a population or the total amount of animals in a given species. An example of this would be the amount of humans around the Earth.

As of June 2026 (The month this article was written), there is around 8.3 billion people inhabiting the planet and that number is projected to increase until 50 years later where the human population will stop growing.

Carrying Capacity

In an environment, there is a certain threshold on how much inhabitants it can hold. This number is called the carrying capacity and is dependent on how much food, water, and space the environment has and makes. An example would be the Amazon Rainforest which potentially has a carrying capcity in the billions as more than 20,000 species live in the rainforest. Once a population has exceeded its carrying capacity, it will start to decrease until it reaches beneath the limit.

Age Structure

The age structure of a population is the distribution of how old or young the individuals in a population is.

Demographic Math

Birth and Death Rates (BR and DR)

In a population, many are born and many die. The amount born out of 1,000 people is called the birth rate and the amount who die out of 1,000 people is called the death rates. Currently in June 2026, the birth rate of humans is slightly bigger than the death rate, but this would change in the future. The formula for both of these is...

Birth Rate :   Number of Births   Total Population  Death Rate :   Number of Deaths   Total Population  Both represented as "x out of 1,000 people".

Crude Birth and Death Rates (CBR and CDR)

The crude birth and death rates also measure the fertility / longetivity of a population. However, unlike regular birth and death rates, crude birth and death rates measure the amount born every 1,000 in a population...

Crude Birth Rate :   Birth Rate (as a percentage)  * 1000 Crude Death Rate :   Death Rate (as a percentage)  * 1000

Population Growth/Decay Rate

Much like the birth rates of a population, the population growth rate measures the speed of which a population grows. But unlike a typical birth rate, a population growth rate measures the populations speed of growth directly instead of a relative measurement of people born out of 1,000 people...

Population Growth Rate :   Crude Births  -  Crude Deaths 10 Population Growth Rate is represented as a percentage (%)

Population Doubling Time

The Population Doubling Time of a population is how much time it takes for a population to double in size. To calculate it, divide 100 ln(2) (or roughly 70) by the rate at which the population grows...

Population Doubling Time:   100  ln ( 2 ) Population Growth Rate (%) = 70 Population Growth Rate (%)

This formula is also called the Rule of 70 as it can be used to easily calculate the Population Doubling Time.
