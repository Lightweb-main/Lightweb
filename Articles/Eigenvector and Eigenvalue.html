Eigenvector and Eigenvalue

Introduction

Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues are special values included in spectral theory.

Eigenvalues

Eigenvalues appear in matrices and in linear transformations. They quantify how stretched an eigenvector is. The formula for finding an eigenvalue is...

Av = λv



A - the matrix

v - the eigenvector

λ - the scalar eigenvalue

In sematic form, the matrix times the eigenvector is equal to the eigenvector times the scalar eigenvalue. An example of this formula being used is this scalar-matrix multiplication problem...

[ 3 2 -4 7 ] [ 2 -3 ]
λ = 4 So matrix A is [ 3 2 -4 7 ] , the eigenvalue is 4, and the eigenvector is [ 2 -3 ] lets see if the two products are equal to each other. If they aren't, then the eigenvector or the eigenvalue is wrong... [ 3 2 -4 7 ] [ 2 -3 ]
λ = 4
