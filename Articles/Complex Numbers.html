Complex Numbers

Introduction

Complex numbers are sums/differences of real numbers with imaginary numbers like these combinations…

5 - 2i 12 - 9i 2i + 3 12i - 8

Standard Form

c = a - bi

Usually parts of a function with complex numbers are shown with Re(x) and with Im(x)...

f(x) = 8i - 6 Re(x) = -6 Im(x) = 8

Adding and Subtracting

When adding and subtracting complex numbers, always add/subtract the values that are real and imaginary separately…

(8 + 2i) - (8i - 5)

First add the negative sign on the second number…

8 + 2i - 8i + 5

Now combine like terms…

(8 + 5) + (2i - 8i) = 13 - 6i

The final answer is 13 minus 6 imaginary. Same thing with addition where we combine like terms…

3i - 2 + 12i + 7 = (3i + 12i) + (7 - 2) = 5 + 15i

The final answer to this problem is 5 plus 15 imaginary.
