Mean Absolute Deviation & Standard Deviation

Introduction

Mean Absolute Deviation (also abbreviated as MAD) and Standard Deviation are both measurements related to mean/average as a way to figure out the differences of value between every value in a set of numbers. Both MAD and Standard Deviation use the mean of a set of numbers to find out their values.

Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD)

Mean Absolute Deviation is the average distance between every value with the mean of the set of numbers. Let's say we had one…

2, 5, 10, 21, 47

We would first find the mean which is every number added up and divided by the amount of numbers so…

2 + 5 = 7

7 + 10 = 17

17 + 21 = 38

38 + 45 = 85

And 85 over 5 equals 17. Now that we have the average of the set, we would have to find the differences for every number to the average. So…

17 - 2 = 15

15 - 5 = 10

15 - 10 = 5

21 - 15 = 6

47 - 15 = 32

And now we would have to add up all the numbers so…

15 + 10 = 25

25 + 5 = 30

30 + 6 = 36

36 + 32 = 68

And now that we have our final number, we would have to divide the number by the amount of numbers in the set again…

68 / 5 = 13.6

So the MAD for this set of numbers is 13.6. The formula for Mean Absolute Deviation is the average of the numbers. The sum of the difference between those numbers. Then you divide the final number by the amount of numbers in the set.
