Rational Equations

Introduction

A rational equation is an equation that has at least one rational expression or a fraction that includes a variable, in the numerator or more commonly in the denominator...

1 x   +   1 4   =   1 5

Solving Rational Equations

To solve a rational equation, find the Least Common Denominator of all the terms. So we would have to find 4 and 5...

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32...

Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35...

We'll also include x

20x (   1 x   )   +   20x (   1 4   )   =   20x (   1 5   )

20   +   5x   =   4x

We'll then subtract 5x on both sides to isolate x on the right...

20   =   -x



x   =   -20

So the supposed value of x is -20. Lets substitute x as -20 to see if it really is -20...

-  1 20   +   1 4   =   1 5

-  1 20   +   1 4   *   5 5   =   1 5   *   4 4

-  1 20   +   5 20   =   4 20

4 20   =   4 20

Since the result on the left is equal to the one on the right, x equals -20.

Here's a more complex rational equation...

3 2r + 4   =   1 2

Looking at the equation, we can't find the LCD of the denominators of both fractions. But we can multiply both sides by (2r + 4) to remove the denominator of the fraction on the left...

 (2r + 4)  3 2r + 4   =    (2r + 4)  1 2

3   =   2r + 4 2

Now with the rational expression on the left gone. Now we can solve the rational equation...

3   =   r + 2

r + 2   =   3

r   =   1

So the supposed value of r is 1, lets see if it's true...

3 2(1) + 4   =   1 2

3 2 + 4   =   1 2

3 6   =   1 2

1 2   =   1 2

Since the values are equal, r has to equal 1/2 or 0.5.

Quadratics from Rational Equations

Quadratics can also appear from rational equations. For example...

x² + 3x + 6 (x - 2)(x + 4)   =   2 (x + 4)

For this rational equation, a quadratic is on top of the fraction on the left. We must first cancel out the (x + 4) on both sides...

x² + 3x + 6 x - 2   =   2

Now that the (x + 4) are canceled out, we now must multiply both sides by (x - 2)...

x² + 3x + 6   =   2(x - 2)

x² + 3x + 6   =   2x - 4

Now lets move all the terms on one side and substitute a zero on the right...

x² + x + 6   =   -4

x² + x + 10   =   0

Now we can use the quadratic formula to find the roots of the quadratic...
