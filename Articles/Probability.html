Probability

Introduction

The Probability of an event/phenomenon is basically how likely it is to occur. So for example, a coin has two sides, heads and tails. The chances of a coin flip landing on tails is 50 percent, which makes the chances of landing on hands also 50 percent.

Theoretical Probability

Theoretical Probability is the probability of an outcome calculated with mathematics. For example, a die (singular for dice) usually has 6 sides, all having 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 dots. Now to calculate the probability of landing on 5, divide the amount of favorable outcomes (in this case 1) by the total amount of outcomes (in this case 6)...
