Parabolas

Introduction

A parabola is a shape commonly found within graphing quadratics and is shaped as a U-shaped curve... A parabolas direction and shape can change vastly depending on the slope of the quadratic that forms it...

General Parabola Formula

The general formulas for parabolas are usually expressed in vertex form which is...

Vertical Parabolas

y = (x - h)2 + k

Horizontal Parabolas

x = (y - h)2 + k

The general formula for a parabola changes depending on the direction of where it's going and where the vertex is...

Parabola Formulas (Vertex is 0, 0)
Parabola Direction Equation
Points Up y2 = 4ax
Points Down y2 = -4ax
Points Left x2 = -4ay
Points Right x2 = -4ay


Parabola Formulas (Vertex isn't 0, 0)
Parabola Direction Equation Vertex Coordinates
Vertical y = (x - h)2 + k (h, k)
Horizontal x = (y - h)2 + k (h, k)


As you can see, the formula of a parabola changes depending on its direction and its vertex coordinates.

Structure of a Parabola

A parabola is made out of many different points and lines that are crucial to actually graphing it.

Vertex

The vertex of a parabola is the highest/lowest point of it. Now the vertex being the lowest or highest point depends on which direction the parabola opens from...
