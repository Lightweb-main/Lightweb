Factorials

Introduction

Factorials are the products of an integer and all the integers under it. An example of a factorial would be…

4!

0! = 1

Every Factorial has an exclamation mark to the right of it. The formula for finding factorials is…

n! = n * (n - 1) * (n - 2)....

So…

4! = 4 * 3 * 2 * 1

= 4 * 3 * 2

= 12 * 2

= 24

By using formulas for factorials, the factorial of 4 is 24. Factorials can also be added up…

5! + 3! = ???

First we use the formula to find the expression for both of the factorials…

(5 * 4 * 3 * 2) + (3 * 2) = ???

= (20 * 6) + 6

= 120 + 6

=126

These two factorials added up equal 126…

5! + 3! = 126

Factorials can also be multiplied together…

(3 * 2) * (4 * 3 * 2) = 144

Factorials can also be divided…

10! / 8! = ???

10! = 10 * 9 * 8 * 7 * 6 * 5 * 4 * 3 * 2

8! = 8 * 7 * 6 * 5 * 4 * 3 * 2

So the final answer is 10 times 9 which is 90…

10! / 8! = 10 * 9
