Euler's Number

Introduction

Euler’s Number is a constant number in mathematics which is used to calculate the natural logarithms of numbers. Euler’s number is approximately 2.71828 and is abbreviated/shown as e in mathematical equations. Euler’s number is similar to pi which are both constant numbers in mathematics. A constant number is a number/symbol in mathematics that has a fixed value meaning that its value doesn’t change no matter what equation it is in.
