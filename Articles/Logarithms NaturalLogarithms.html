Logarithms: Natural Logarithms

Introduction

Natural Logarithms are log equations that have Euler’s Number (Shown as e) as the base. Natural Logarithms are used to accurately estimate exponential growth. Examples of Natural Logarithms are…

loge(x) = 3

This isn’t the only way that Natural Logarithms are written.

ln(1) = 0

Remember that any logarithm with a base of 1 always equals zero.

eln(x) = x

Above me is the formula for natural logarithms.

loge(5) = ln(5)
