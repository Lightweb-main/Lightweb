Quadratic Mean

Introduction

A quadratic mean (also called Root Mean Square) is another type of average that measures the absolute magnitude of a set of numbers.

Formula

To get the Root Mean Sqaure from a set of numbers, there are a few steps you would need to do...

1. Multiply each number in the data by itself.

2. Find the average of all the numbers

3. Sqaure root the average

∑ x i 2 n Example An example of finding a quadratic mean would be a set of numbers like this one…

10, 11, 11, 16, 20, 25

The first step is to square every number in the set and add them up…

102 = 100

112 * 2 = 121 * 2 = 242

162 = 256

202 = 400

252 = 625

100 + 242 = 342

342 + 256 = 598

598 + 400 = 998

998 + 625 = 1623

Next is to find the mean of those squares which can be done by adding them up and dividing the sum (1623) by the amount of numbers in the sum (6)...

1623 / 6 = 270.5

The final step is to square root the mean to get the Quadratic Mean…

RMS2 = 270.5

RMS = √270.5

The final quadratic mean of this set of numbers is the square root of 270.5 or approximately 16.4469.
