Distance vs Displacement

Introduction

Distance and Displacement are both mesaurements on how much land that has been covered. They are similar to each other but also have distinct differences.

Distance

Distance is the total amount of land that has been covered by an object. Distance can have twists, turns, curves, and straight lines. Distance can also include land that has been covered twice... For example, we can take a batter in a baseball game, the pitcher throws a curveball but the batter successfully hits it and makes a home run... Lets use the measurement that the distance from a base to another is 90ft. And the batter went to the 1st base, then to the 2nd, then to the 3rd, then lastly stopped at home base...
