Probability Distribution

Introduction

Probability Distribution in statistics is a description for a variable, basically teliing how much outcomes are favorable in the variable. For example, take a dice for example, a dice usually has 6 sides, every sides probability is 1/6 since there's only one iteration of it and there are 6 of them in total. This is what is known as discrete probability distribution where the group of possible outcomes is finite or countable.

Probability Distribution Rules

Even though there are many types of probability distribution, there are some rules they all need to meet. First, all of the probabilities need to be greater than or equal to 0 since they cannot have a negative probability. And second, the sum of all the probabilities needs to equal 1, this is becuase 1 equals 100% and events cannot happen 101 percent of the time for example.
