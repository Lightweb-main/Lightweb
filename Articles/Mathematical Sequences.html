Mathematical Sequences

Introduction

Mathematical Sequences are a list of numbers that are in order and have a set rule that every term follows...

{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7...}

Sequences Notation

Sequences are noted as a list of items all seperated by a comma (,) with {curly brackets} around the sequences...

{1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 12, 17, 23}

The Structure of a Sequence

Every sequence usually has a rule, which is a constant formula used to find the next value of every term by a relative term...

Rule = 2n

{1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128}

Every individual 'object' in a sequence is called a term. So for the sequence above, the 1st term is 1, the 4th is 8, and the last term (9th) term is 128.
