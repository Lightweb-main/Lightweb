The Pythagorean Theorem

Introduction

The Pythagorean Theorem is an equation that can help find the hypotenuse of a triangle…

a2 + b2 = c2

The Hypotenuse

The hypotenuse of a right triangle is the longest side… The hypotenuse’s length is the root of c2 in the Pythagorean Theorem.

Examples

The first example of the pythagorean theorem is a triangle with a length of 5 and with a width of 3… A is the smallest side of the triangle which is three in this example.

32 = 9

B is the longer side relative to A but not bigger than the hypotenuse (c)...

52 = 25

Using the pythagorean theorem formula…

9 + 25 = 34

c2 = 34

We would have to square root both c and 34 to get the actual length of the hypotenuse…

c = √34

The length of the hypotenuse is the square root of 34… But what if the hypotenuse and one of the sides are known…

c2 - a2 = b2

48 - 16 = 32

b2 = 32

b = 32 ≈ 5.656

The unknown side of this triangle is approximately 5.656 or the square root of 32…
